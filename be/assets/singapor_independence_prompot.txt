Under the scorching midday sun, a bustling farm prepares for the upcoming Feast of the Hungry Ghosts. A teenage boy, taking a brief respite from work, happily carries his baby brother outside, their rare moment of play amidst the busy harvest. Nearby, the boy’s colleague nervously cycles to deliver orders, avoiding intimidating foreign households. The vibrant yet complex social tapestry of 1950s Singapore unfolds—diverse cultures, languages, and hierarchies clashing in everyday life. In the markets, children return from school as laborers pause for lunch, reflecting the struggles of survival and identity in a rapidly changing society.
